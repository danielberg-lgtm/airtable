Why now

Three signals point to the same moment.

The global marketing organization now runs brand, product, sponsorship, and always-on work across every region at once. Each demand on its own is manageable. Together they demand one connected system.

5

Five workstreams, one org

Brand platforms, product launches, sponsorship activations, always-on campaigns, and regional adaptions all run in parallel across every market. Each has its own plan, agency, and channel mix, with no shared view of the whole.

Portfolio load
30+

A brand platform at scale

Always-on platforms like Priceless run alongside anniversary moments, sponsorships, and category launches year after year. Orchestrating that breadth across every market cannot be done from decks and email threads.

Brand at scale
All

Agents entering the workflow

From agentic commerce to AI assisted creative and media, agents are moving into how marketing gets done. An agent ready org needs structured, connected data it can see, trust, and act on across every region.

Working with agents
The problem

Planning is everywhere. The truth is nowhere.

Campaign work is spread across regional hubs, agency partners, and channel teams. Each keeps its own version of the plan in its own format. Nobody has the full picture, and leadership is flying blind between status meetings.

01

Content waste across regions

Markets recreate assets they cannot see, duplicating production and burning budget on work that already exists somewhere else in the org.

02

Brand dilution at the edges

Without centralized oversight, messaging and visuals drift across regions. Customers encounter a fragmented brand instead of one Mastercard.

03

Regions not learning from each other

What works in one market stays trapped there. Wins are not replicated and mistakes are repeated, because no one can see across the whole.

04

Slow approvals, missed moments

By the time a campaign clears review and a cultural moment passes, the opportunity is gone. Leadership sees status in a weekly deck, long after the window moved.

The Airtable point of view

You cannot run agents on marketing data that lives in decks and spreadsheets.

Every ambition on the roadmap, from brand platforms to product launches to always-on campaigns, eventually asks the same question of marketing: where is the single source of truth?

Today the answer is a patched together collection of files. AI cannot reason across it, agents cannot act on it, and leadership cannot trust it. The work is rich. The substrate is not.

Airtable is the connected operating layer that turns fragmented marketing work into structured, linked, live data. Campaigns, assets, regions, budgets, and approvals become records that relate to each other, update in real time, and feed the dashboards and agents on top.

One system, strategy to execution

A single connected record from planning through production, activation, and reporting. Not just the planning data.

Built for the people doing the work

Intuitive and visual enough that marketers and creatives actually adopt it, with speed to value in weeks not months.

Brand consistency, by design

AI content automation keeps messaging aligned across channels, with a dedicated portal for external agencies.
